A Moving Madison: A Visual Narrative, Print

Inspired by Rebecca Solnit's city atlas collection, we wanted to create a map of Madison depicting movement between the glacial lakes, through the isthmus, and around the drumlins. Everyday Madisonians are navigating the terrain that was created thousands of years ago by the movement of a glacier that came to a halt just outside Madison (depicted in the lower left hand corner.) The light frosty blue illustrates the extent of the glacier, while some of the isthmus drumlins almost mimic the isthmus orientation and direction of congested traffic coming through Highway 151, Willy St, and Johnson St. Overlayed bus stops emphasize our grid-like movements amongst this organic backdrop.

This map was initially accompanied by an essay and constructed with the help of Lucienne Argent, Michael Brooks Arnsteen, and Laura Szymanski. Since then, it has been slightly modified for another class to be a stand alone map.
