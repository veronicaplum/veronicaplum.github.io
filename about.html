Veronica Plum

I’ve always found myself to be highly adaptable with a diverse array of interests. Varying from photography, cartography, satellite data, videos, to a desire to travel, I tend to be a visual thinker. I was born in Northern Bemidji, Minnesota, but have also lived in Urban parts of Wisconsin and Tianjin, China (one of the country's largest cities). And I wouldn't consider myself a country or city person. I enjoy both perspectives and see a need for me personally and global functionality. This mentality has kept me actively curious and open to new experiences that motivate me to explore new places whenever given the chance.

I have a passion for telling stories, specifically when highlighting different perspectives defined by spaces. I’ve always been taught that if their is an unconventional way to tell a story, that the unconventional way will be more intriguing than a conventional essay. These interests eventually lead me to pursue a degrees in Human Geography, discussing geographic theories of multiplicity, and Cartography/GIS, giving me a medium to convey information in the way I saw most effective.

While I have a degree in human geography, I have found my interests drifting toward people - environment geography. Many of my mapping projects have discussed water issues, Chinese politics, and climate change. In the future, I’d like to work more with information on oceanography and marine life, and assessing change detection in these areas through remote sensing applications.

My sense of adventure has placed me in a constant state of movement, but when I’m not studying geography or brainstorming the next map idea, I’m either behind a camera, walking in nature, or planning my next trip.
